Chapter 2 · The funding gap

83.6% of all VC went to all-male teams in 2024

Of $289 billion invested globally, all-male founding teams captured $241.9B. All-female teams received just $6.7B — 2.3 cents of every dollar printed. The press runs almost entirely on blue ink.

Chapter 3 · Representation

Only ~13% of unicorn founders are women

Women are 43% of global entrepreneurs, yet among the founders of billion-dollar startups, barely one in eight is a woman. That share dropped from 13% in 2017 to just 6% in 2021 before a modest rebound.

Chapter 5 · The hidden edge

Female-founded unicorns reach $1B faster

The press can be recalibrated.

The data is clear: female-founded startups deliver outsized returns relative to capital deployed. Structural changes — not goodwill — are what rebalance the machine.

◈
More female VC partners
Firms with female partners invest 2.3× more in female founders. Today only 15.4% of decision-makers are women.
◈
Measure exits, not just deals
Female-founded companies made up 24.3% of US VC exits in 2024 — a record. Returns-based metrics tell a fairer story.
◈
Diverse boards
57 Indian unicorns had zero women on their boards. Structural governance gaps have measurable business costs.
◈
Sector-specific funding
FemTech and DeepTech are proving grounds where female-founded companies consistently outperform benchmarks.
